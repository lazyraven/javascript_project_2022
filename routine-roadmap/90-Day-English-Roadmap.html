Method

The 7 core steps

01

Listening

  • Weeks 1–4: BBC Learning English, English with Lucy, Speak English with Vanessa
  • Later: TED Talks, podcasts, interviews, documentaries
  • Method: listen once without pausing → listen again carefully → repeat what you hear
02

Shadowing

  • Pick a 1-minute video. For every sentence: play → pause → repeat exactly
  • Copy pronunciation, speed, intonation, emotion, and pauses — not just the words
This is the highest-leverage 20 minutes of your day. If you only have time for one activity, choose this.
03

Speaking Practice

  • Morning: "Today I have office." · "I need to finish my tasks." · "I have a meeting at 11."
  • While cooking: "I'm cutting vegetables." · "I'm making tea." · "The food smells delicious."
  • While walking: describe what you see — "I can see a white car." · "People are walking quickly."
Goal: speak continuously, out loud, without stopping to correct mistakes.
04

Vocabulary Building

  • Learn by topic, not at random: Week 1 Office, Week 2 Travel, Week 3 Shopping, Week 4 Technology
  • For every word capture: meaning, pronunciation, an example sentence, a synonym, an antonym
  • Example: instead of "good" → excellent, outstanding, fantastic, impressive, exceptional
05

Grammar Revision

  • Month 1: present, past and future tenses; modal verbs (can, could, should, would, must)
  • Month 2: conditionals, passive voice, reported speech, relative clauses
06

Reading

  • Read aloud daily from BBC News, Medium articles, The Guardian, or beginner-friendly novels
  • Read slowly — pay attention to pronunciation, punctuation, and intonation
07

Writing

  • 10 minutes daily in a journal: "Today I completed my tasks." · "I learned Vue.js." · "Tomorrow I will practice speaking."

Added · Professional Goal

Mock interview practice

The plan mentions "practice interviews" in Month 3 — here are 5 real questions to rehearse out loud, with a model answer shape. Tap to expand.

Tell me about yourself. +

Structure: current role → 1–2 relevant achievements with a number → why you're interested in this move. Keep it under 60 seconds — this isn't your full history, it's a trailer.

Why do you want to leave your current company? +

Stay forward-looking: talk about what you're moving toward, not what you're escaping. Avoid criticizing your current employer directly.

Describe a challenge you faced at work. +

Use STAR: Situation, Task, Action, Result. End on the result with a measurable outcome if you can — "reduced load time by 40%," not just "it worked well."

Where do you see yourself in five years? +

Connect your growth to the kind of work the role offers, so the answer feels relevant to them, not just to you.

Do you have any questions for us? +

Always have 2–3 ready: about the team's current priorities, how success is measured in the role, and what a typical first quarter looks like.

The Arc

The 90-day plan

MONTH 01

Build the Foundation

  • Improve everyday conversations
  • Revise essential grammar
  • Learn 250–300 useful words
  • Speak for 2–3 minutes continuously
MONTH 02

Improve Fluency

  • Discuss work and technology
  • Improve pronunciation and intonation
  • Learn advanced sentence structures
  • Speak for 5–7 minutes confidently
MONTH 03

Speak Like a Professional

  • Practice interviews
  • Participate in workplace discussions
  • Deliver short presentations
  • Speak naturally for 10 minutes without hesitation
Consistency matters more than intensity. Practice a little every day — that's the whole plan.
